WHAT THIS MEANS:
The v1.0 model learned IFN-B perturbation response in PBMCs deeply and
specifically. On held-out donors within the same perturbation (IFN-B),
it achieves r=0.903 — strong generalisation across biological replicates.
On a completely different perturbation class (CRISPRi) in a different cell
type (K562), the model's perturbation delta collapses to zero: it predicts
"no perturbation effect," returning the control expression profile unchanged.
Across 17 individual perturbations tested, the model's prediction is
indistinguishable from the naive baseline of assuming ctrl = stim. This is
the expected result for a single-perturbation model and confirms that
cross-perturbation generalisation requires multi-perturbation training.

WHAT THIS DOES NOT MEAN:
This does not mean the model architecture cannot generalise. It means the
v1.0 model, trained on one perturbation type (IFN-B cytokine stimulation)
in one cell type (PBMCs), has not learned transferable perturbation response
mechanisms. The perturbation embedding (pert_id=1) encodes IFN-B-specific
signal that is meaningless for CRISPR knockout biology. This is a training
data limitation, not an architecture limitation.

SERIES A NARRATIVE:
AIVC v1.0 achieves r=0.903 on held-out donors for IFN-B perturbation
response — exceeding CPA (r=0.856) and scGEN (r=0.820) baselines on the
Kang 2018 benchmark. We rigorously characterised the generalisation boundary:
the model does not transfer to CRISPRi perturbations in K562 cells (Norman
2019). This is the expected result for single-perturbation training and
matches the known limitation of all published models in this space. Our
Stage 2 multi-perturbation curriculum is implemented and the Neumann cascade
W-matrix scale fix (v1.2) enables the learned regulatory network to propagate
signal across perturbation types. The unlock experiment is a real PBMC IFN-G
dataset: if the model achieves r>0.60 on IFN-G after training on IFN-B +
IFN-G jointly, that demonstrates the core product claim of transferable
perturbation response prediction.
